I'd love to hear from you!

I'm currently in the process of applying to Ph.D. programs in the hopes of working on neuroprosthetic devices! I've previously worked on projects ranging from in vitro testing of biocompatibility of electrodes on neuronal-like cells with an engineer specializing in active medical implants, to troubleshooting and testing a slow-scan voltammetry setup on awake mice in a neurobehavior lab, to a swine model of coronary artery disease and diabetes in a translational physiology lab. These labs have allowed me to develop a foundational skillset and understanding of a range of research topics and approaches that I will apply to earning a Ph.D. focusing on sensorimotor pathways associated with neuroprosthetic devices. To further explore my experiences, download my C.V. here or view it at the bottom of the research page.
